Immediate danger

If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you are not in immediate danger but need to report a crime, use your local agency’s non-emergency contact.

Harassment, stalking, threats

  1. Preserve evidence: screenshots, message exports, URLs, and dates/times. Write a short timeline.
  2. Secure your core accounts: email + phone number + Apple/Google. See Device Security.
  3. Report via official channels: contact your local law enforcement agency (non-emergency if appropriate). Use official websites to find current contact details.
  4. Consider protective steps: document incidents, review home privacy (address exposure), and share your situation with a trusted person.

Fraud & identity theft

Start with the FTC resources—they provide a step-by-step plan and documents you can use with banks and credit bureaus.

  • IdentityTheft.gov — recovery plan & documentation
  • ReportFraud.ftc.gov — report scams and fraud

If money accounts are involved: change passwords, enable MFA, and contact your financial institutions using known-good contact methods (official app/website statements, not links in emails/texts).

Cybercrime reporting

If you experienced a cyber-enabled crime (account takeover, scam, wire fraud, marketplace fraud, etc.), start with local law enforcement and also consider reporting through the official cybercrime reporting channels below.

  • FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) — federal cybercrime complaint portal

State resources (optional)

Many states maintain their own reporting portals, consumer protection pages, and cybersecurity guidance. Search your state name + “consumer protection” and your state name + “cyber crime reporting” for official links.

Crisis support (US)

If you’re in immediate danger call 911. If you need crisis support, these are official national resources:

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • The National Domestic Violence Hotline

Families & kids

  • School info: avoid publicly posting schedules, teacher names, and pickup routines.
  • Photo safety: avoid house numbers, street signs, and identifying landmarks.
  • Talk about scams: local buy/sell group scams and “urgent payment” requests are common.
