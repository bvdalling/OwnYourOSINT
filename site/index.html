Practical security & privacy for everyday life

This is a plain-English guide for normal people. It focuses on practical steps that reduce risk from harassment, identity theft, account takeover, and oversharing—without needing to be a tech expert.

Quick wins (15 minutes)

  • 1 Turn on device updates (iOS/Android, Windows/macOS).
  • 2 Enable MFA (prefer an authenticator app over SMS).
  • 3 Lock down social profiles and remove your home address from public posts.
  • 4 Stop password reuse (use a password manager).

Local reality: small communities amplify exposure

In smaller communities, a little information goes a long way. Your name + school + employer + “Logan UT” can be enough to find addresses, relatives, old usernames, and photos.

  • Reduce your “join points”: don’t use the same username everywhere.
  • Limit address clues: avoid posting street signs, house numbers, or routine routes.
  • Use layers: lock accounts, limit public info, and secure devices.
