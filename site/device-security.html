Device Security

Your phone and email are the keys to everything. These checklists focus on “high leverage” settings that prevent most real-world takeovers.

Phone checklist (iPhone & Android)

  • Update the OS and enable automatic updates.
  • Lock screen: strong passcode, disable lock-screen notifications for sensitive apps.
  • App permissions: review Location, Microphone, Camera, Contacts; remove what isn’t needed.
  • Remove unknown profiles (especially on iOS) and unknown admin apps (Android).
  • Backups: ensure backups are on and you can restore them (Apple/Google).
  • SIM security: ask your carrier about a port-out/SIM-swap protection PIN.

Computer checklist (Windows/macOS)

  • Updates: enable automatic OS + browser updates.
  • Disk encryption: FileVault (macOS) / BitLocker (Windows) if available.
  • Browser hygiene: remove suspicious extensions; keep only what you use.
  • Separate accounts: use a non-admin user for daily activity when possible.
  • Backups: Time Machine / Windows Backup; test a restore path.

Home Wi‑Fi & router basics

Your router is a “shared door” into every device on your home network. If you only do a few things, prioritize these.

  • Change the router admin password (don’t leave the default).
  • Update router firmware (many routers have an “Update” section in settings).
  • Use strong Wi‑Fi encryption (prefer WPA3 if available; otherwise WPA2).
  • Turn off remote management unless you truly need it.
  • Create a guest network for visitors / smart devices when possible.

Official guidance: CISA: Home Network Security

Safe browsing & public Wi‑Fi

  • Keep browser extensions minimal; remove ones you don’t recognize.
  • Be careful on public Wi‑Fi: avoid sensitive logins when possible.
  • Prefer your phone hotspot for banking or account recovery flows.

Official guidance: CISA Secure Our World

If you feel monitored or harassed

If you think you’re being stalked, digitally harassed, or threatened, your top priority is safety. Avoid “DIY forensics” that could destroy evidence or escalate conflict.

  • Preserve evidence: screenshots, URLs, dates/times, messages. Export where possible.
  • Secure your email: change password + enable MFA; review forwarding rules.
  • Change key passwords from a trusted device/network.
  • Tell a trusted person and create a check-in plan.
  • See Resources for reporting and support pathways.
